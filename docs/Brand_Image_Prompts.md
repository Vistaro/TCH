# TCH Brand Image Prompts

These are prompts to feed to ChatGPT (or any image-generation model) to create
the imagery the public TCH website needs. Generated images go in
`public/assets/img/site/` with the filenames listed below — the page CSS and
markup already references them, so dropping the file in is enough.

## Cast & demographic guidance (read first — applies to every prompt)

- **Caregivers**: predominantly **Black South African women**, age range
  25–55. South Africa is roughly 87% Black so the imagery should reflect that.
  A small minority of caregivers can be Black men or other ethnicities for
  variety, but the dominant face of the brand is Black women.
- **Clients**: typically **older White / Afrikaans South Africans**, age range
  60–85. This matches the actual customer demographic of in-home care services
  in Gauteng. Some clients can be families with younger members (e.g. an adult
  daughter arranging care for her elderly mother).
- **Tone**: warm, dignified, calm, trustworthy. **Never** sad, clinical, or
  posed/corporate. Real-life moments — a hand on a shoulder, a shared laugh, a
  cup of tea, helping someone walk in a garden.
- **Setting**: comfortable South African suburban homes — not hospitals, not
  clinics. Natural light wherever possible. Think Pretoria/Centurion suburbs:
  spacious lounge, garden, kitchen.
- **Wardrobe**: caregivers in clean, simple uniforms (light blue or teal scrubs,
  or a smart polo with a small badge). Clients in everyday casual clothes.
- **Branding**: do **NOT** include any text, logos, watermarks, or signage in
  the images. We'll add the TCH branding in CSS overlays.
- **Aspect ratios**: noted per image.
- **Style**: photorealistic, NOT illustrated, NOT cartoon, NOT 3D-rendered.
  Should feel like a high-end documentary photograph or stock photo from a
  premium brand campaign.

## Generation tips for ChatGPT / DALL-E / Midjourney

- **Use the entire prompt verbatim**, don't trim. The specificity is what
  prevents generic stock-photo blandness.
- **Generate 4 variants** of each image and pick the best, rather than asking
  for one and accepting it.
- **Reject anything that looks staged, corporate, or "model-y"**. We want
  photographs that feel like they could have been taken by a documentary
  photographer in a real home, not a brochure shoot.
- **Reject anything where the caregiver looks subservient** — the relationship
  should feel like respectful partnership, not hierarchy.
- **Reject anything with visible signage, logos, brand names, or text** —
  even on packaging in the supermarket image.
- **Watch for subtle anatomy issues** that AI image models often introduce
  (extra fingers, weird hands, mismatched eyes). Regenerate until clean.
- **Once finalised**, export each image as a JPEG at the resolution noted
  above and save with the exact filename listed. Drop into
  `public/assets/img/site/` and upload to the dev server at
  `~/public_html/dev-TCH/dev/public/assets/img/site/`.
